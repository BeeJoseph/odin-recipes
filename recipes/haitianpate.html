Haitian Pate

Description

All you need is two squares of a chicken filled Haitian pate to make you full. Enjoy this in place of a everyday-average sandwhich at work for a quick savory pick-me-up. After eating this, you may favor the crust over the meat flling, or vice versa. Whichever it is, you won't be able to put any part of it down.

Ingredients

Filling

  • Ground meat (of your choice)
  • Onion
  • Habanero pepper
  • Thyme
  • Parsley
  • Rosemary
  • Lime juice
  • Salt

Dough

  • Cold water
  • All-purpose flour
  • Salt
  • White vinegar
  • Lard
  • Egg yolk

Steps

  1. First, prepare the filling. In mortar pound to paste the meat, parsley, thyme, rosemary, scotch bonnet pepper, shallot, and garlic.
  2. Over medium heat, in a skillet or saucepan, Heat olive oil. Add onions and seasoning mixture. Stir-fry for 1 minute until softened.
  3. Add the meat and simmer covered over medium heat for about 10 minutes with lemon juice and 1/2 c of water white vinegar, freshly ground black pepper and Adobo seasoning salt until meat is tender and water is absorbed. Stir constantly. Filling is ready when all liquid is absorbed.
  4. Add tomatoe paste to mixture
  5. Mix salt in. Knead dough and wrap into plastic and separate balls of 16. Put into fridge.
  6. Roll out the doughs into rectangles.
  7. Put the filling into the doughs and wrap and close with a fork.
  8. PLace into an oven of 400 degrees for 45 minutes until golden brown.
  9. Remove and brush them with egg wash.
  10. Serve and enjoy.
